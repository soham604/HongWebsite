## What Is CMHC Multifamily Insurance?

Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC) offers mortgage loan insurance for rental properties with **5 or more units**. This insurance protects lenders against borrower default, which allows them to offer significantly better terms than conventional financing.

For Quebec investors, CMHC-insured financing is one of the most powerful tools available. It reduces your required down payment, extends your amortization period, and typically offers lower interest rates than conventional mortgages.

## Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for CMHC multifamily insurance, your property must meet several criteria:

- **Minimum 5 residential rental units** — this is the threshold that separates multifamily from residential
- **Property must be in Canada** and meet CMHC's physical condition standards
- **Minimum Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) of 1.1x** — meaning the property's net operating income must cover at least 110% of the mortgage payments
- **Environmental assessment** may be required depending on the property
- **Property appraisal** from a CMHC-approved appraiser

In Quebec specifically, properties must comply with the Régie du logement regulations, and rental income projections must reflect Quebec market rents.

## Down Payment: As Low as 5%

This is the headline benefit. With CMHC insurance, you can acquire a multifamily property with **as little as 5% down**. Compare that to conventional financing, which typically requires 20-25% down for the same property.

On a $1,000,000 six-plex, that is the difference between putting down $50,000 and $200,000. That freed-up capital can go toward your next acquisition or property improvements.

## Amortization: Up to 50 Years

Through the **CMHC MLI Select** program, qualifying properties can access amortization periods of up to 50 years. Standard CMHC-insured multifamily loans typically offer up to 40 years, and MLI Select extends this further for properties that meet specific social, accessibility, or climate compatibility criteria.

Longer amortization means lower monthly payments, which improves your cash flow from day one. A 50-year amortization versus a 25-year amortization can reduce your monthly payment by roughly 30%.

> **MLI Select Scoring:** Properties earn points for affordability (rents below median market rent), accessibility (barrier-free units), and energy efficiency (GHG reductions). Higher scores unlock longer amortization and lower insurance premiums.

## Insurance Premiums

CMHC charges an insurance premium based on the loan-to-value ratio and the amortization period. Premiums typically range from **2.6% to 6.15%** of the loan amount. This premium can be added to the mortgage, so you do not need to pay it upfront out of pocket.

While the premium adds to your total cost, the math usually works in your favour: the lower interest rate you receive with CMHC insurance often more than offsets the premium cost over the life of the loan.

## Interest Rates

Because CMHC insurance eliminates lender risk, insured multifamily mortgages typically carry interest rates that are **50 to 150 basis points lower** than conventional rates. On a million-dollar mortgage, that can save you $5,000 to $15,000 per year in interest.

## Quebec-Specific Considerations

- **Régie du logement:** Quebec's rental regulations affect how quickly you can increase rents, which impacts your proforma projections
- **Municipal taxes:** Property transfer taxes (welcome tax) in Quebec are calculated on a progressive scale and can be significant on multifamily transactions
- **Language requirements:** Documentation and tenant communications in Quebec must comply with language laws
- **Energy efficiency:** Quebec's climate programs can complement MLI Select scoring for better terms

## When CMHC Makes Sense

CMHC-insured financing is not always the right choice. It works best when you want to **maximize leverage**, preserve capital for multiple acquisitions, or when the property meets MLI Select criteria for extended amortization. If you are buying a property that needs significant renovation, conventional financing with a value-add strategy may be more appropriate.

The best approach is to run the numbers both ways — CMHC-insured versus conventional — and see which path delivers better returns for your specific situation.
